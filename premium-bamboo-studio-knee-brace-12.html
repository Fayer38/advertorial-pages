Your Knee Brace May Be Making Things Worse

Here is what no one tells you at the doctor's office. Your rigid brace may be starving the joint of what it needs most.

"Knee cartilage has no direct blood supply. It relies entirely on surrounding tissue perfusion for nutrients. Any device that restricts local blood flow — including rigid braces — can slow the very recovery patients hope to accelerate." — Journal of Orthopaedic Research

Your knee cartilage is like a sponge. It absorbs nutrients and expels waste through compression and release during movement. When a rigid brace restricts micro-movements, that pumping action stalls. Inflammatory byproducts accumulate. Synovial fluid thickens. Cartilage cells receive less oxygen. The joint deteriorates faster, not slower. This is why your knee feels better in a brace but worse over months.
