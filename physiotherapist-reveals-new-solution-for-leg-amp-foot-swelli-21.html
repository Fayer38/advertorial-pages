The Real Reason Your Nerves Won't Calm Down

Your damaged nerves aren't the whole story. There's a feedback loop making everything worse — and most treatments completely ignore it.

"Peripheral neuropathy creates a chronic stress response. Damaged nerves send distress signals to the brain, which elevates cortisol, which inflames nerve tissue further, which amplifies pain signals. It's a vicious cycle — and until you interrupt the cortisol-inflammation loop, no amount of medication will provide lasting relief." — Dr. Rebecca Thornton, Neurophysiology Researcher

Think of your nervous system like a smoke alarm stuck on. The original damage — from diabetes, chemotherapy, or aging — triggered it. But your stress response keeps re-triggering it. Cortisol floods your system nightly, inflaming sensitive nerve endings. Your brain interprets normal sensations as burning and tingling. The nerves aren't worsening — your body's response to them is.
