What Mainstream Coverage Quietly Skips

Here is what surprised me. Most consumer health articles describe neuropathy as a vague nerve issue, then pivot to prescription drugs that dull the signal. Almost none explain the wiring underneath, or why so many adults develop these symptoms in midlife without an obvious cause.

Peripheral nerves rely on a fatty insulation called the myelin sheath. When that sheath thins or frays, signals leak, misfire, or cut out, which the brain interprets as burning, tingling, or numbness. Three neurologists I spoke with described it, plainly, as old wiring in a busy house.

The conventional response treats the noise rather than the cable. Painkillers and nerve blockers can mute symptoms, but they do not feed the sheath, support repair, or address fiber density. That gap is exactly where targeted nutrient support, the boring kind, has quietly been gathering evidence.
