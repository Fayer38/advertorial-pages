"It's Not Just Pain — It's Your Whole Life Disappearing"

If you're living with neuropathy, you already know this. But I'm going to say it anyway, because nobody else seems to understand.

It's not "just" tingling. It's dropping your grandchild's bottle because your fingers went numb without warning. It's not trusting your own feet on the stairs. It's canceling dinner plans because by 6 PM, the burning is so bad you can barely think.

The worst part? The nighttime. I'd lie in bed with my feet feeling like they were wrapped in barbed wire and dipped in ice — simultaneously burning and freezing. My husband moved to the guest room because I couldn't stop shifting and wincing all night.

I Tried Everything. Here's What Failed Me.

Over six years, I became a walking pharmacy experiment. My neurologist put me on gabapentin first. It took the edge off the pain — but replaced it with a fog so thick I couldn't remember my own zip code. I gained 15 pounds in two months.

Then came pregabalin. Same fog, plus dizziness so bad I fell in my kitchen and bruised my hip. My doctor's solution? "Let's try a lower dose."

I tried capsaicin cream. It burned my skin but didn't touch the nerve pain underneath. I tried B12 injections — expensive, painful, and the relief lasted maybe two days. I tried acupuncture, essential oils, ice baths, heating pads.

Each time something new failed, a little more hope died.

The worst moment came last Thanksgiving. My granddaughter Lily — she's four — reached up and grabbed my hand to pull me to the table. I couldn't feel her fingers. I looked down at our hands together and felt absolutely nothing.

I excused myself, went to the bathroom, and cried for ten minutes.

That was the night I decided I was done accepting "manage your symptoms" as an answer. There had to be something — anything — that addressed why my nerves were deteriorating, not just masked the pain while they kept getting worse.
