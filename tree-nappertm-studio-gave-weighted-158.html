Why Most Weighted Blankets Fail Hot Sleepers

Here is the part nobody talks about when they sell you a weighted blanket: the weight was never the problem.

Most weighted blankets use glass microbeads or plastic pellets sewn into polyester shells. They deliver pressure — but also trap body heat against your skin. For anyone who runs warm at night, especially women navigating hormonal shifts, this does not calm the nervous system. It overwhelms it. You get the weight without the relief.

Your body needs to drop one to two degrees in core temperature to initiate deep sleep — a process called thermoregulation. When a blanket traps heat, it blocks this cooling. Your nervous system stays activated, cortisol stays elevated, and the calm you were chasing never arrives. The material — not the pounds — determines whether deep pressure stimulation works.
