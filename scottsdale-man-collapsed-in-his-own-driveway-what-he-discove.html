Scottsdale Man Collapsed in His Own Driveway — What He Discovered Next Made His $400 Blower Obsolete

Home & Garden Correspondent

📷 Média

Dave Moreno ignored the warning signs for years. Every Saturday morning, the retired HVAC technician from Scottsdale would drag his 23-pound Husqvarna backpack blower out of the garage, strap it on, and spend the next hour clearing his driveway and patio. At 52, the dull ache in his lower back had become so routine he barely noticed it anymore. He'd pop two ibuprofen, stretch for a minute, and power through. That's what guys like Dave do — they push through the pain because the yard isn't going to clean itself. His wife Karen had been telling him for months to see a doctor. He kept putting it off. The leaves kept falling. And every Saturday, that ache got a little worse.

📷 Média

Then came that October afternoon. Dave was halfway through his fall cleanup, the Husqvarna rumbling against his spine, when a sharp, electric pain shot down his left leg like a bolt of lightning. His knee buckled. He went down hard on the concrete, the blower still strapped to his back, leaves swirling around him. Karen found him lying on the driveway, unable to move. The emergency room visit led to an MRI, which led to a referral to Dr. Patel at Banner Health. The orthopedic surgeon didn't sugarcoat it. He pulled up Dave's scans and said something Dave will never forget: 'Men over 45 who repeatedly handle vibrating equipment over 15 pounds are three times more likely to develop chronic lumbar radiculopathy. And you're already there.'

📷 Média

What followed was eight weeks of physical therapy, three times a week. Ice packs every night. A bill that climbed to $4,200 out of pocket after insurance. Dave couldn't mow, couldn't rake, couldn't even bend over to pick up a fallen branch without wincing. His yard — the one thing he'd always kept pristine — started looking neglected. Leaves piled up along the fence. The patio turned brown with dirt and debris. For a man who'd spent 30 years working with his hands, who'd built and maintained HVAC systems across the Southwest, watching his own property fall apart felt like a personal failure. He swore he'd never pick up that gas blower again. But he had no idea what he'd use instead.

📷 Média

The answer arrived at the most unexpected moment. It was a family barbecue in late December, and Dave was sitting in a lawn chair, carefully avoiding any sudden movements. His son-in-law Marcus walked over with something that looked like an oversized hair dryer. 'Hey Dave, try this,' Marcus said, handing it to him. Dave took it with one hand and almost laughed out loud. At 2.1 pounds — lighter than a bag of sugar — he was convinced it was a toy. Some kind of novelty gadget from an infomercial. 'You're joking, right?' Dave said. Marcus just smiled and pointed toward the patio, where wet leaves from the morning rain were matted to the concrete. 'Just try it,' he said. Dave shrugged, aimed the nozzle, and pulled the trigger.

📷 Média

The leaves didn't just move. They launched. A 200 MPH jet of concentrated air ripped the wet, stuck-on leaves off the concrete like they weighed nothing. Dave stood there, one hand holding the device, the other hand holding his beer, watching in disbelief as the patio cleared in under three minutes. No vibration rattling his spine. No 23-pound monster strapped to his back. No starter cord to yank. Just a quiet, cordless tool that weighed less than his morning newspaper and hit harder than his $400 Husqvarna. The device was called the Seese Pro, and Dave's first words after clearing the patio were: 'Why does this exist and why didn't anyone tell me sooner?'

The Seese Pro runs on a brushless motor — the same technology found in high-performance drones and electric vehicles. That motor spins at over 30,000 RPM, generating up to 1,200 CFM of airflow through a precision-engineered nozzle. For context, most gas-powered backpack blowers max out around 500 CFM. The brushless design also means no carbon brushes wearing down, no friction-based heat buildup, and a motor lifespan roughly three times longer than conventional alternatives. The 21V lithium-ion battery delivers around 40 minutes of runtime per charge — more than enough to clear a 400-square-foot patio, dry a full-size sedan, and still have power left over. The entire unit weighs 950 grams. That's 2.1 pounds. Dave's orthopedic surgeon reviewed the specs and told him it was well within safe parameters for daily use.

📷 Média

But what surprised Dave most wasn't the raw power. It was how many tools the Seese Pro quietly replaced. His leaf blower, obviously — the Husqvarna now sits untouched in the garage. But also his chamois cloths for drying the truck after a wash. His old plastic ice scraper that never worked properly on Arizona's rare frost mornings. Even the compressed air cans he used to clean dust from his workshop electronics. One tool, four jobs. Dave started keeping the Seese Pro on a hook by the back door, the way you'd hang a set of keys. 'I use it almost every day now,' he told me. 'Pollen on the porch furniture? Ten seconds. Grass clippings on the walkway? Thirty seconds. I grab it, blast whatever needs blasting, and put it back. The whole thing takes less time than tying my shoes.'

📷 Média

Karen noticed the change before Dave did. Not just in the yard — in Dave himself. He wasn't grimacing when he stood up from the dinner table. He wasn't avoiding weekend chores or making excuses about the weather. The guy who'd spent two months feeling useless was back to maintaining his property with visible pride, only now he was doing it without pain and in a fraction of the time. Their neighbor, a 67-year-old retired teacher named Gloria, watched Dave clear her sidewalk one afternoon and asked where she could get one. She'd been paying a lawn service $80 a month just for leaf removal. Gloria ordered a Seese Pro that evening. Two weeks later, she canceled the service. She told Dave it was the first time in years she'd felt truly independent in maintaining her own home.

📷 Média

The Seese Pro currently retails at $119.99 and ships with a lithium-ion battery, a fast charger, and a carrying case included at no extra cost. There's a 30-day satisfaction guarantee — full refund, no questions asked — plus a lifetime replacement warranty on the motor. For Dave, the math was simple. He'd spent $4,200 on physical therapy from an injury caused by a heavy blower. He'd spent $400 on the blower itself, plus gas and maintenance every season. The Seese Pro cost him nothing — Marcus bought it as a gift — but even at full price, it would have paid for itself before the first bag of leaves hit the curb. If you've been putting off yard work because the tools are too heavy, too loud, or too much hassle, it might be worth seeing what 2.1 pounds of brushless engineering can do. Dave's only regret is that he didn't find it before his back gave out.
