That was the moment I knew something had to change.

The Problem Nobody Talks About: Your Yard Tools Are Wrecking Your Body

Here's what I didn't realize until my physiotherapist spelled it out for me: the average gas-powered leaf blower weighs between 10 and 23 pounds. Hold that weight at arm's length, add the vibration of a combustion engine, and you've got a recipe for back strain, shoulder damage, and chronic joint pain.

And it's not just me.

Everything I Tried Before — And Why None of It Worked

I'm not the kind of person who gives up easily. So before I found the real solution, I went through the full checklist of bad alternatives.

The broom. My wife suggested I just go back to sweeping. Sure — if I wanted to spend 25 minutes hunched over a patio, pushing wet leaves that stick to concrete like glue. My shoulders ached almost as much as with the blower. And the driveway? Forget it.

The "lightweight" electric blower. I bought one from a big-box store. It weighed 7 pounds — better, but still heavy enough to strain my arm after 15 minutes. And the power? Pathetic. It could barely move dry leaves, let alone wet ones matted to the ground. I returned it after a week.

Hiring a lawn service. I looked into it. The quotes came back at $150 to $200 a month just for leaf removal. That's over $1,000 a year for something that should take me minutes. It felt absurd.

Ignoring the problem. I tried that too. My neighbor politely mentioned that leaves from my yard were blowing into his. My HOA sent a letter. Turns out, you can't just... not do it.

So there I was. Stuck between tools that hurt my body and alternatives that didn't actually work.
