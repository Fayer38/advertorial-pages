Four Common Causes of Chronic Lower Back Pain

Before I found what worked, I had to understand what was actually happening in my back. My physical therapist explained that chronic lower back pain usually falls into one of four categories — and most treatments only address the symptoms, not the root cause.

  • DEGENERATIVE DISC DISEASE The cushioning discs between your vertebrae gradually wear down with age, reducing shock absorption and causing persistent stiffness and aching that worsens with movement throughout the day.
  • SCIATICA NERVE COMPRESSION A herniated disc or bone spur presses against the sciatic nerve, sending shooting pain down one leg that can make even sitting in a chair unbearable.
  • SPINAL STENOSIS NARROWING The spinal canal narrows over time, squeezing the nerves inside and creating a heavy, tired feeling in your legs that makes walking even short distances exhausting.
  • MUSCLE WEAKNESS INSTABILITY When deep core and back muscles weaken from disuse or pain avoidance, the spine loses its natural support system, leaving you vulnerable to recurring flare-ups and stiffness.

What Most People Try First and Why It Falls Short

The standard path is familiar to anyone who has lived with back pain. You start with over-the-counter painkillers. When those stop working, you move to a chiropractor — adjustments that feel wonderful for a day, maybe two, then the pain settles right back in. Physical therapy helps while you're doing it, but the relief fades the moment you stop going. TENS units send electrical pulses that mask the pain temporarily, like turning up the radio so you can't hear the engine knocking.

Meanwhile, the underlying problem — my weakened back muscles — wasn't being addressed at all. Nothing was actually making me stronger. Everything was just buying time.
