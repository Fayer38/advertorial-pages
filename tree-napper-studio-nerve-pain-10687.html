What's Really Happening Inside Your Damaged Nerves

🖼️
Emplacement média — media.s1
"Diagram-style illustration of nerve fibers in a human hand showing peripheral ne"

Peripheral neuropathy occurs when the nerves outside your brain and spinal cord become damaged. These peripheral nerves send constant misfiring signals — tingling, burning, stabbing pain — even when nothing is actually wrong.

Your nervous system is essentially stuck in alarm mode. The nerves that should be sending normal sensation signals are instead broadcasting pain, numbness, and that maddening pins-and-needles feeling.

Over time, your brain starts interpreting all touch as potentially threatening. A bedsheet brushing your feet. Socks against your toes. Even the pressure of standing becomes a source of pain.

Meanwhile, your body's stress response kicks into overdrive. Cortisol levels spike. Serotonin drops. Sleep becomes nearly impossible. And without quality sleep, nerve repair slows to a crawl.

It's a vicious cycle: damaged nerves cause pain, pain causes stress, stress blocks healing, and the damage gets worse. Breaking this cycle is the key that most treatments completely miss.
