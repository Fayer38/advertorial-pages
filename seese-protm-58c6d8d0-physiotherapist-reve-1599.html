Why most cordless blowers still hurt

The dirty secret of the cordless blower category nobody wants to print on the box

A cordless blower's listed weight is measured without the battery. Once you clip in the 4Ah or 5Ah pack most people buy, you're often holding three or four extra pounds — all of it behind the handle. That's where the shoulder pain comes from, not the advertised number.

Four of the five blowers I tested listed weights between 3.8 and 5 pounds on the box. Add a standard battery and those numbers jumped to between 6.2 and 8.4 pounds on my kitchen scale. Worse, the weight sits behind the wrist, so every minute of use multiplies the strain on the rotator cuff and forearm.

Why each alternative ultimately failed Gary

  • The Ryobi 40V is genuinely powerful, but with its included battery it clocked 6.8 pounds on my scale. Gary set it down after ninety seconds of hover time.
  • The Worx WG545 is a clever little handheld, but the airflow disappointed on our wet November leaves. It blew dust around without moving the actual pile we built.
  • The Greenworks 24V felt lighter in hand, but its battery drained in eleven minutes on high. For our quarter-acre, that meant stopping twice mid-driveway to swap packs.
  • The second Ryobi model, a larger backpack-style, was a nonstarter the moment Gary tried to lift it onto his right shoulder. My granddaughter Maddie couldn't lift it either.

The one that actually passed

So what makes a cordless blower something a recovering shoulder and a nine-year-old can both operate for fifteen minutes?

Two design choices almost nobody else makes: the motor sits forward of the handle, and the battery is integrated, not clipped on behind.

Balance point matters more than weight.

Forward-balanced motors aren't new — professional landscapers have used them in gas blowers for decades. What's new is seeing that layout in a unit light enough for a 75-year-old woman. When the center of mass sits over your hand instead of behind it, the effective lifting effort drops by nearly half, even at the same total weight.

  • On my kitchen scale it weighed 2.1 pounds with the battery in, confirmed — less than half of what the Ryobi 40V registered. Gary held it one-handed at chest height for seven minutes before his shoulder asked him to stop. That was the longest he'd managed in two years.
