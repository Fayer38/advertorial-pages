I Tried Everything. Nothing Worked.

Over the years, I went through the usual cycle. You probably have too.

First, I tried a corded electric blower. Lighter, sure. But dragging 100 feet of extension cord around the yard — getting it tangled in bushes, tripping over it on the driveway — that's not a solution. That's a different kind of misery.

Then I tried a battery-powered blower from a big-box store. It was cheaper, cordless, and I thought I'd finally found the answer. But it barely moved dry leaves, let alone wet ones. It felt like pointing a hair dryer at my patio. I returned it after a week.

I even went back to basics. A push broom for the patio. Towels for the car. A leaf rake for the yard. My wife suggested it. "Less machinery, fewer problems," she said.

She wasn't wrong about the machinery part. But 25 minutes of sweeping a 400-square-foot patio is not "fewer problems." It's just a different kind of pain — slower, quieter, and just as exhausting.

Here's what I finally realized: the problem wasn't me. It wasn't my age, my back, or my motivation.

The problem was that every tool on the market forced me to choose between power and pain. Heavy and effective, or light and useless. There was nothing in between.

Or so I thought.
