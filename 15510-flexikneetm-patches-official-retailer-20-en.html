The Pharmacology

Here is what pharmacists know but rarely volunteer at the counter when you grab another family-size bottle.

Ibuprofen, naproxen, and aspirin block an enzyme called COX-1 that protects your stomach lining from its own acid.

Block that enzyme for months and the lining thins, then erodes, then bleeds — often without any warning pain.
