Everything I Tried (And Why None of It Worked)

Let me save you some time and money. Here's what I spent six years — and over $4,700 — trying:

Over-the-counter painkillers. Ibuprofen, naproxen, acetaminophen. They'd dull the pain for a few hours, then it came roaring back. My doctor warned me about liver damage if I kept going at the rate I was taking them. So that was a dead end.

Prescription anti-inflammatories. Stronger pills, same story. Plus stomach problems I'd rather not describe.

Topical creams and gels. I had an entire drawer full of them. Voltaren, Bengay, Biofreeze, some "miracle" cream from an Instagram ad that cost $65 and smelled like turpentine. Temporary relief at best. Most did nothing at all.

Physical therapy. Helpful in theory. But $150 per session, twice a week? My insurance covered 12 visits. Then I was on my own — and the stiffness came right back.

Paraffin wax baths. Messy, time-consuming, and the relief lasted maybe 20 minutes.

Cortisone injections. A needle directly into my knuckle joints. If you've had one, you know. The relief lasted about three weeks. Then I needed another one. My rheumatologist said I could only get three per year.

Nothing addressed the root issue. Everything was a temporary band-aid that either wore off, caused side effects, or drained my bank account.

I was exhausted. Not just physically — emotionally. I'd started turning down invitations. Stopped gardening, which I loved. Even stopped writing birthday cards to my grandkids because holding a pen for more than a minute was agony.

I was 58 years old and felt like I was 85.

And then, in early December, I ran into Carol.
