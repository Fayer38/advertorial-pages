Why Nerve Pain Gets Worse at Night

Peripheral neuropathy affects roughly 20 million Americans. The damaged nerves in your hands and feet misfire constantly, sending pain, tingling, and numbness signals that your brain cannot ignore — especially when everything else goes quiet at bedtime.

During the day, your brain is busy processing a thousand other inputs. But the moment you lie down, those misfiring nerves become the loudest signal in the room. That is why so many neuropathy sufferers dread the hours between 10 PM and 2 AM.

Most doctors reach for gabapentin or pregabalin first. These drugs work by dampening your entire nervous system — which is why patients report feeling foggy, disconnected, and sometimes more fatigued than they were before treatment started.
