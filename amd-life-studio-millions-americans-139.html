Here is what Karen's doctors never explained clearly. The pain in your lower back is almost always a symptom, not the disease. The real issue is that the deep stabilizing muscles surrounding your lumbar spine have weakened to the point where they can no longer do their job.

When those muscles atrophy, your vertebrae lose their support structure. Discs compress unevenly. Nerves get pinched. The body compensates with inflammation, which creates the pain signal. Every painkiller, every injection, every heating pad addresses the signal while the structural problem quietly gets worse underneath.

This is why so many treatments provide temporary relief but never lasting results. They are treating the alarm, not the fire. Strengthening those deep muscles is the only path to genuine improvement. But traditional exercise is too painful for people in chronic distress, and physical therapy requires a consistency that life does not always allow.
