The Night I Almost Gave Up on Sleep Entirely

My name is Claire. I'm 34, and for the past three years, sleep has been my biggest enemy.

It started slowly — a few restless nights here and there after a stressful week at work. Then it became every night. I'd crawl into bed exhausted, but the moment my head hit the pillow, my brain would ignite. Thoughts spiraling. Heart racing. Body overheating.

By morning, I felt worse than when I went to bed.

And I know I'm not alone in this. According to the American Sleep Association, 50 to 70 million American adults have a sleep disorder. That's not a small number. That's an epidemic hiding in plain sight.

Warning: Chronic sleep deprivation doesn't just make you tired — it's linked to weakened immunity, weight gain, anxiety, and long-term cognitive decline. If you've been running on broken sleep for months, your body is keeping score.

The worst part? Everyone around me seemed to sleep just fine. My husband would be snoring within five minutes. Meanwhile, I was lying there like a boiled lobster under our comforter, too hot to stay covered, too anxious to sleep without weight on my body.

I started to dread bedtime. The one part of the day that's supposed to be rest became the part I feared most.

I Tried Everything. And I Mean Everything.

Over three years, I became a walking encyclopedia of sleep hacks. Here's what I tried:

Sleep apps and meditation. I downloaded four different ones. The ocean sounds were nice, I guess. But they didn't stop me from overheating at midnight and ripping the covers off.

CBD gummies. Expensive. Tasted decent. Didn't change a thing about my actual sleep quality.

A $200 cooling pillow. Cooled my head, sure. But the rest of me was still a furnace.

My first weighted blanket. This one hurt the most — because it almost worked. The first night, I felt that deep, gentle pressure and thought, "This is it. This is the answer." I fell asleep in minutes.

Then I woke up at 1 AM absolutely drenched. Sweat on my neck. Sweat behind my knees. The blanket felt like it weighed a hundred pounds and radiated heat like a car hood in July.

I tried it for two more weeks, desperately hoping I'd adjust. I didn't. It went into the closet. Then the donation bin.

And I told myself: "Weighted blankets aren't for hot sleepers. Period."

I was wrong.
