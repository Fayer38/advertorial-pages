What The Keratin Barrier Actually Does

"Cross-section medical illustration of a human toenail showing the keratin nail p"

Your toenail is built from densely packed keratin, the same protein that makes up your hair and the outer layer of your skin. It's designed to be tough — to protect the delicate nail bed underneath from pressure, friction, and infection.

When fungus settles in, it tucks itself between the nail plate and the nail bed, in a sealed pocket where moisture and warmth feed it. Anything you brush onto the top of the nail has to physically pass through that wall.

Most home remedies and even many drugstore antifungals never do. They sit on the surface, evaporate, get washed off in the shower, and leave the colony underneath completely untouched. That's the gap almost no bottle on the shelf addresses honestly.

Why Nightly Application Matters

Nail tissue is slow tissue. A toenail grows roughly one-tenth of an inch per month, which means visible change is gradual by definition. Any product promising overnight transformation is selling you something the biology of your foot cannot actually deliver.

Done every evening for ninety days, the active stays in continuous contact with the keratin barrier instead of being washed away. Most users describe the first visible change at the base of the nail, where new growth comes in clearer.
