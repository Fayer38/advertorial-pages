Scottsdale Man Collapses in His Own Driveway — What He Discovered Next Is Saving Backs Across America

Home & Garden Correspondent

📷 Média

Dave Moreno spent 28 years crawling through attics and hauling compressors across Phoenix rooftops. His back took every hit without complaint. So when the retired HVAC technician from Scottsdale turned 52 and started feeling a dull ache every Saturday morning, he did what most men his age do — he ignored it. Every weekend, same routine: drag the 23-pound Husqvarna backpack blower out of the garage, strap it on, and spend 45 minutes fighting leaves, dust, and desert grit off his property. His wife Karen asked him to slow down. His doctor told him to be careful. Dave shrugged it off. He'd carried heavier equipment for three decades. What was a leaf blower going to do? He was about to find out.

📷 Média

One October afternoon, mid-cleanup, it happened. Dave was sweeping the blower across his front walkway when a sharp, electric pain shot from his lower back straight down his left leg. His knee buckled. He hit the concrete driveway hard, the blower still rumbling on his back. Karen found him there ten minutes later, unable to stand. The emergency room visit led to an MRI, which led to a referral to Dr. Raj Patel at Banner Health in Scottsdale. Dr. Patel didn't sugarcoat it. He pulled up the scan and pointed to Dave's L4-L5 disc. Then he said something Dave will never forget: 'Men over 45 who repeatedly handle vibrating equipment over 15 pounds are three times more likely to develop chronic lumbar radiculopathy — and you're already there.'

📷 Média

What followed was eight weeks of physical therapy, three sessions a week. The total cost: $4,200 out of pocket after insurance. Dave couldn't mow, couldn't blow leaves, couldn't even wash his truck without wincing. The yard he'd always kept immaculate started looking neglected. Neighbors noticed. Dave noticed them noticing. For a man who spent his whole career fixing things, not being able to maintain his own property felt like a personal failure. He tried hiring a landscaping crew — $180 a month for basic cleanup. He tried a lightweight electric blower from the hardware store — barely strong enough to move dry grass clippings. He even tried a leaf vacuum, which clogged every ten minutes. Nothing worked. And every failed attempt reminded him of what Dr. Patel had said: your back cannot handle this anymore.

📷 Média

Then came Thanksgiving weekend. Dave's daughter and son-in-law, Tyler, drove down from Flagstaff for the holiday. After dinner, Tyler noticed the pile of leaves banked against the garage door. 'Hey Dave, you want me to take care of that?' He walked to his truck and came back holding something that looked, to Dave, like a cordless hair dryer. It was matte black, compact, and weighed — Dave checked — just 2.1 pounds. 'It's called a Seese Pro,' Tyler said. Dave literally laughed out loud. 'That little thing? Come on.' Tyler just smiled, pointed it at the leaf pile, and pulled the trigger. The blast hit 200 miles per hour. Wet, matted leaves that had been cemented to the concrete for weeks launched off the surface like they'd been hit by a pressure washer. Dave stopped laughing immediately.

📷 Média

Dave grabbed the Seese Pro from Tyler's hand and held it up. One hand. 950 grams — less than a bottle of water. He pointed it at the driveway and swept the remaining debris in a single pass. No strap digging into his shoulders. No vibration rattling his spine. No gasoline smell. No pull cord. He cleared the entire front walkway, the patio, and the garage entrance in under six minutes. His back felt nothing. Absolutely nothing. He stood there for a moment, staring at this little device, and felt something he hadn't felt in months: capable. 'I turned to Karen and said — why didn't anyone tell me about this?' Dave told me when I interviewed him three weeks later. 'I spent $4,200 on my back because of a problem a $119 tool could have prevented.'

📷 Média

Here is why something this small hits this hard. The Seese Pro runs on a brushless motor — the same core technology used in commercial drones and Tesla drive systems. It spins at 30,000 RPM and pushes air at up to 1,200 CFM, which is more than double the output of most gas blowers on the market. Traditional blowers use brushed motors that create friction, generate heat, and waste energy on vibration. That vibration is exactly what destroys spinal discs over time. The brushless design eliminates mechanical contact inside the motor, which means virtually zero vibration reaches your hand, your arm, or your back. At 200 MPH wind speed, the Seese Pro matches or exceeds the raw output of backpack blowers that weigh ten times as much. The engineering is simple: compress more air through a tighter space, faster. No weight required.

But Dave quickly discovered this was not just a leaf blower. In December, he used it to clear frost and light snow off his truck windshield in 40 seconds — no scraping, no cold fingers. In January, he blew sawdust and debris out of his workshop after a shelving project. In February, after washing his Silverado, he dried the entire truck in under three minutes — zero towels, zero water spots, zero contact with the paint. One device replaced his gas blower, his chamois cloths, his shop vac attachment, and his ice scraper. Four tools gone, one tool in. His garage shelf cleared up. His Saturday routine dropped from 45 minutes of back-straining labor to about 8 minutes of effortless, one-handed work. Dave now uses the Seese Pro almost daily. The battery lasts 40 minutes on a single charge — which covers roughly eight to thirteen full tasks before needing the included fast charger.

When I asked Dave about the price, he did the math out loud. His old Husqvarna cost $389 new. Gas and two-stroke oil ran about $15 a month. A chamois and drying towels — $45 a year. The ice scraper and shop attachments — another $50 combined. Physical therapy after the blower wrecked his back — $4,200. The Seese Pro costs $119.99, down from its regular price of $249.99 — a 55 percent discount that the manufacturer says is temporary. It ships with a hard carrying case, the rechargeable lithium-ion battery, a fast charger, and three bonus accessories valued at $120. It is backed by a lifetime replacement warranty and a 30-day satisfaction guarantee. If it does not work for you, send it back. Full refund, no questions. Dave spent more on two months of yard service than this tool costs outright.

📷 Média

Three months after that Thanksgiving moment, Dave's yard looks better than it has in years. He does his own cleanup again. His back has not flared once. He told me he even caught his 71-year-old neighbor, Frank, eyeing the Seese Pro over the fence last week. Dave handed it to him. Frank cleared his entire porch one-handed in four minutes and tried to buy it on the spot. Dave ordered him one that night. 'I'm not exaggerating when I say this thing gave me my independence back,' Dave told me. 'That sounds dramatic for a blower, I know. But when you've been told your body can't handle the work anymore, and then something this simple proves that wrong — it changes how you feel about getting older.' If your current yard tools are costing you more in pain than they're worth, the Seese Pro might be worth 30 risk-free days of your time.
