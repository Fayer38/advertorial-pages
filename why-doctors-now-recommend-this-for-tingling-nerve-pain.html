THE REAL PROBLEM

Here's what most neuropathy treatments get wrong: they focus exclusively on the nerves themselves. Pain medication numbs the signal. Supplements try to repair the myelin sheath. But neither addresses the environment those nerves are trying to heal in.

When your body is stuck in a chronic stress state — elevated cortisol, suppressed serotonin, fragmented sleep — your peripheral nerves cannot repair themselves. It's like trying to fix a road while traffic is still running over it.

A 2023 study published in the Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine found that patients with peripheral neuropathy who achieved deeper, more restorative sleep showed measurably slower nerve degeneration compared to those with disrupted sleep patterns. The connection was striking: better sleep didn't just improve how patients felt — it appeared to slow the progression of the disease itself.
