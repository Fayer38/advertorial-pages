The Silent Epidemic Nobody Talks About at Brunch

Here's what I didn't know back then: I wasn't alone. Not even close.

According to the National Sleep Foundation, 67% of women report waking up too hot at least a few nights per week. And among women aged 25 to 45 — my exact demographic — that number climbs higher when you factor in stress, hormonal shifts, and the compounding effect of chronic sleep debt.

But nobody talks about it. We joke about needing coffee. We laugh about being "not a morning person." We push through.

Meanwhile, the research is clear: sleeping less than six hours a night increases cortisol by up to 37%, impairs decision-making at levels comparable to being legally intoxicated, and — the one that hit me hardest — makes you measurably more irritable with the people you love most.

Warning: Chronic sleep deprivation doesn't just make you tired — it compounds. Every restless night raises baseline stress hormones, which makes the next night even harder. Researchers call it the "sleep debt spiral," and most people don't realize they're in one until the effects show up in their health, their mood, and their relationships.
