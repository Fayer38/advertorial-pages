How NMES technology works

Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation (NMES) is a well-established modality used in physical therapy and rehabilitation. It delivers controlled low-voltage electrical impulses through surface electrodes to stimulate motor nerves, which causes targeted muscle contractions. Unlike TENS — which primarily stimulates sensory nerves to modulate pain perception — NMES is intended to activate and recondition the muscle itself.

  • Electrodes placed on the skin transmit low-level impulses to motor nerves.
  • The nerves trigger controlled contractions in targeted muscle groups.
  • Over repeated sessions, this may support muscle reconditioning and activation.
  • In the lower-back region, the goal is to engage deep stabilizing muscles (multifidus, transverse abdominis) associated with postural support.

NMES is used in clinical and at-home settings. Outcomes depend on device parameters, consistency of use, individual condition, and adherence to the manufacturer's instructions. It is not a substitute for professional medical care.

Safety information & contraindications NMES devices are not suitable for everyone. Do not use without first consulting a qualified healthcare provider if any of the following apply to you:
  • You have a pacemaker, implanted defibrillator, or any other active implanted electronic device.
  • You are pregnant or nursing.
  • You have a history of epilepsy, cardiac arrhythmia, or active malignancy.
  • You have broken skin, recent surgical incisions, or active skin infections at the application site.
  • You are undergoing treatment for a serious medical condition.
Do not apply electrodes over the carotid sinus region, eyes, or mouth. Stop use immediately and consult a physician if you experience discomfort, skin irritation, or any unusual symptoms.
