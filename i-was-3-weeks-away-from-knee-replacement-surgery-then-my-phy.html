Everything I Tried — And Why None of It Lasted

If you're reading this, I'm guessing you've been down the same road I have. Let me save you the suspense: I tried everything.

Ibuprofen three times a day — until my doctor warned me about stomach bleeding and kidney damage. After 18 months of daily use, my GI tract was a mess.

Cortisone injections — two rounds. The first one gave me three good weeks. The second barely lasted ten days. My doctor said I couldn't keep getting them. Too much cortisone breaks down cartilage even faster.

Physical therapy — I went twice a week for four months. It helped with mobility, sure. But the deep, aching pain? Still there. Every single morning.

Glucosamine supplements — I took them religiously for a full year. I wanted so badly to believe those capsules were doing something. But honestly, I couldn't tell the difference.

Copper knee sleeves from the internet — comfortable, yes. But pain relief? Minimal at best.

Turmeric, collagen powder, CBD cream, acupuncture, even a $400 electromagnetic pulse device I saw on a late-night infomercial.

Nothing worked long enough to matter.

My husband Richard finally said what I didn't want to hear: "Carol, maybe it's time to just do the surgery."

So I agreed. I signed the consent forms. I picked out a walker.

And then — three weeks before my surgery date — I went to my last physical therapy session.
