Everything I Tried Failed — Until I Stopped Thinking Like a Consumer

Over the next two weeks, I ordered five different blowers. Cordless ones. Lightweight ones. The ones with the fancy ergonomic handles.

The "lightweight" models? Most still weighed 8 to 12 pounds. That's fine for a healthy 35-year-old. But for a 59-year-old woman with tennis elbow, it's still too much.

And the truly light ones — the little handheld fans — couldn't blow a napkin off a table.

I spent forty years as an engineering consultant. I know motors. I know airflow dynamics. And I knew the real problem wasn't the blower market — it was the motor technology everyone was using.

Standard brushed motors are cheap, but they're heavy, they wear out fast, and they waste energy on friction instead of airflow.

So I asked myself: what if I used the same brushless turbine technology they put in high-performance drones?
