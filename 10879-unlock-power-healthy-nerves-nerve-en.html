Why Your Nerves Keep Getting Worse

Here is what most doctors never explain to their neuropathy patients, even though the science has been available for years.

"Peripheral neuropathy is not simply a pain condition. It is a progressive degenerative process in which the protective myelin sheath around nerve fibers breaks down, nerve fiber density decreases, and signal transmission becomes impaired. Treating only the pain while ignoring the underlying nerve deterioration is like putting tape over a check engine light."

Your peripheral nerves are wrapped in a protective coating called the myelin sheath. When this coating degrades, exposed nerve fibers misfire, sending pain, tingling, and numbness to your brain. Over time, unprotected fibers shrink and die. This is why symptoms worsen. The burning and tingling you feel are distress signals from nerves losing their protective insulation and deteriorating.
