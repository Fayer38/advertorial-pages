Everything Walt Tried — And Why Nothing Worked

After his knee surgery, Walt didn't just give up. He fought like hell.

First, he tried a lighter electric blower from Home Depot. Still 11 pounds. After 15 minutes, his shoulders were on fire and his knees were screaming from standing on concrete.

Then he tried a rake and broom. The bending. The twisting. He threw his back out twice in one month.

He hired a landscaping crew — $160 a month for someone to spend 20 minutes with a gas blower. It felt like robbery, but what choice did he have?

He even tried one of those cheap handheld blowers from Amazon. "Felt like blowing through a straw," Walt told Greg. "Couldn't move a wet napkin off the porch."

And every week, the leaves kept falling. The pollen kept settling. The car kept needing drying after every wash.

But here's what really ate at Walt: he didn't want someone else doing it. He wanted to do it himself. That's who he was. A man who took care of his own property. A man who didn't ask for help.

That identity was slipping away — one painful weekend at a time.
