Looking back, the warning signs were everywhere — quiet little things I blamed on age, on retirement, on a long Arizona summer.

Waking up at 3 a.m. with a pounding heart and an inexplicably dry, parched mouth.

A soft roll of weight around my middle that no walk and no salad ever seemed to budge.

Sudden, almost panicked hunger an hour after a sensible lunch — the kind that makes you raid a pantry.

My primary doctor called it 'mild' and told me to lose twenty pounds. The visit lasted under seven minutes.

Nobody ever asked what my mornings actually felt like, or why a black coffee could light my blood sugar on fire.
