Why Everything You've Tried Hasn't Worked

If you've been dealing with knee pain for more than a few months, chances are you've been through what doctors call the "pain management carousel."

First, there are the over-the-counter painkillers. Ibuprofen, acetaminophen, naproxen — they dull the pain for a few hours, but they don't fix anything. Long-term use? The FDA warns it can damage your liver, kidneys, and stomach lining. You're not healing. You're just masking the damage while it gets worse underneath.

Then come the cortisone injections. Your doctor tells you they'll "calm the inflammation." What they don't always mention is that a 2019 study in the journal Radiology found that repeated cortisone shots actually accelerate cartilage loss. The very thing that's causing your pain — they're making it worse, faster.

Next, someone suggests physical therapy. And yes, PT can help — but at $150 to $350 per session, two to three times a week, for months? Most insurance caps out long before you see real improvement. And the moment you stop going, the pain creeps right back.

Finally, there's the nuclear option: knee replacement surgery. A $30,000 to $50,000 procedure with a 3-to-6-month recovery. You can't drive for weeks. You'll need someone to help you bathe. And here's the statistic surgeons rarely volunteer: 1 in 5 patients reports being dissatisfied with the outcome.
