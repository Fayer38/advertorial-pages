Your Hands Aren't Just Inflamed — They're Mechanically Unsupported

Here's what nobody told you. The pain in your hands isn't just inflammation. It's what happens to your joints every hour without proper structural support.

According to rehabilitation researchers, arthritic joints without consistent graduated compression experience accelerated cartilage erosion because synovial fluid — your body's natural lubricant — cannot distribute evenly. The inflammation you feel is your body's alarm that the joint is grinding itself down.

Think of your finger joints like hinges. When surrounding tissue is inflamed, those hinges lose alignment. Without support, every movement creates uneven friction. That friction triggers more inflammation, more swelling, more friction — a self-reinforcing cycle. Pills and creams interrupt pain signals temporarily but do nothing to stop the mechanical grinding that restarts the cycle within hours.
