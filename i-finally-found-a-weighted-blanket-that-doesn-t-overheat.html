Why Most Weighted Blankets Overheat

Not all weighted blankets are created equal. The fabric and construction determine whether you'll sleep cool or wake up in a puddle. Understanding these four categories helped me finally find what works.

  • POLYESTER FILL BLANKETS The most common and cheapest option. Polyester traps body heat like plastic wrap, creating a microclimate that gets hotter throughout the night. Most complaints about weighted blankets overheating trace back here.
  • COTTON WEIGHTED BLANKETS Better than polyester, but cotton absorbs moisture without releasing it quickly. You end up sleeping on damp, warm fabric that clings uncomfortably by morning.
  • GLASS BEAD FILLERS Some brands use glass beads instead of plastic pellets for weight. They conduct heat away from the body better, but the outer fabric still matters more than the filler inside.
  • EUCALYPTUS FIBER CONSTRUCTION The newest approach uses eucalyptus-derived fibers that actively wick moisture and allow airflow. Combined with an open-knit design, this is the only category that genuinely stays cool through the night.
