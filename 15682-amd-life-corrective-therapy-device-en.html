The Hidden Cause

Deep along the base of your spine sits a small, ribbon-like muscle called the lumbar multifidus.

It is the only muscle whose entire job is to stabilize each individual vertebra during every movement you make.

When your back is healthy, this muscle fires automatically, hundreds of times an hour, without you noticing.

But within seventy-two hours of your first acute back episode, the nervous system shuts the multifidus down as a protective reflex.

And here is the part nobody warned you about: that shutdown does not switch back on by itself.

Years later, while you are sweating through bird dogs and bridges, your multifidus is still neurologically offline.

You were never lazy. You were performing surface exercises on a deep muscle that could not even hear the command.
